Churches of the A1

From London to Edinburgh — 410 miles of ancient stones, soaring spires, and a thousand years of faith

✟

The A1 is more than a road. It is a spine of stone that has carried pilgrims, kings, and armies for two millennia — from the Roman legions who first carved it into the landscape, to the medieval travellers who stopped to pray at wayside chapels, to the modern drivers who pass, often unknowing, the most remarkable collection of sacred architecture in Britain.

This is a guide to those churches. Not every chapel and meeting house, but the ones that matter — the ones that have shaped history, sheltered saints, and stood witness to the long story of these islands.

Featured Church

St Wulfram's, Grantham

Lincolnshire — Mile 110

A cathedral in all but name. St Wulfram's 282-foot spire has guided travellers on the Great North Road since the 14th century. Its gargoyles are a gallery of medieval face-pulling; its library, chained books from 1598.

Margaret Thatcher was baptised here. Isaac Newton worshipped here as a schoolboy. The church has seen 700 years of the road pass by its door.
